Problems

CodeSprint will have a mix of algorithmic and Real World Problems.

As the name implies, Real World Problems are challenges that CodeSprint companies have actually faced and solved (in the engineering sense of the word). The end result is a problem set that contains more problem variety and test fidelity; scores more accurately reflect the value an applicant will bring to a company.

The addition of Real World Problems increases problem variety to the point where not all problems are relevant to all positions, and indeed some problems will be disproportionately important to some companies. Companies will be able to weight problems according to position relevance, and find uniquely qualified candidates for their unique positions.

Scoring

The point values for problems will be determined by the formula 100 * (1 - p), where p = [# of programmers who solved this problem] / [# of programmers who solved at least one problem] Thus, the more people who are able to solve the problem, the fewer points it will be worth.

Problems are scored by their ability to pass a secret set of test cases. We provide a set of sample test cases and outputs for programmers to test their formatting and reveal some (but not all) edge cases.

We do not consider code elegance or code length for the score, but companies will have access to your solutions.
